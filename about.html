About Us

Fox Child Care and Development Foundation – Uganda (FOCARE)

Organization was established by Mr. Ogwang Richard Gabriel and concerned community members in Okorom village, Ngora district. It started its operations in 2017 having studied and observed the plight and challenges faced by orphaned and vulnerable children (OVC) in Teso sub region.
The challenges mostly are;

  • The number of children (OVC) is in the increase. This has placed heavy burden on the widows and caretakers.

  • Child labor and harmful traditional practices Child trafficking

  • Increased number of idle no-work children, no schooling, Early child marriages, Child mothers and child abuse

  • Child headed families, Children having in the house hold headed by an elderly person (grandfather)

  • Children at a high risk of suffering from poor physical and mental health or early childhood death from HIV/AIDS infection, homeless, vagrancy and poor parental skills later in life.

ABOUT US

WHY [FOX CHILD CARE-FOCARE]

The organizations name is rooted from Mr. Alan Fox an English businessman who has generously offered his services to transform generations in activism and advocacy in Uganda. He has in many cases supported children’s education and societal transformation in Uganda. Indeed, he is remembered for his help.

However, he has trusted Richard Ogwang and his team in Uganda to ensure that disadvantaged children get necessary assistance helping to ensure community empowerment for sustainable development.

OUR ORGANISATION STRUCTURE

OUR VISION

To bridge the vulnerability gap by providing children equal access to social support for a better life.

OUR MISSION

To inspire breakthroughs

in the way the community treats vulnerable children and to achieve immediate and long-lasting change in their lives.

OUR OBJECTIVES

The overall goal of Fox Child Care and Development Foundation Uganda is to promote and advance the education of people living in or originating from Teso- Uganda and to develop the capacity of people living in or originating from Teso – Uganda in such a way that they are better able to identify and help meet their needs and to participate more fully in society to achieve sustainable development

ACCOUNTABILITY FRAMEWORK

We adopted an Organizational Accountability Framework that guides our approach on how we understand accountability and help us answer the following questions;To what extent are we making progress against our impact goals and shape priorities? How vibrant is our network overall and where are the areas for improvement in our organizational development? How well are we collaborating with key stakeholders and engaging key constituents? How do they regard?To what extent are we modeling the principles of transparency, feedback and participation in our internal ways of working and externally.

We define each element of our Accountability Framework as such:
Impact: FOCARE and our partners’ contribution to bringing about sustainable change in the fight against poverty which in many times brings vulnerability and injustice, specifically through our commitment to support the most vulnerable children in providing social support .

Organizational Performance: FOCARE commits to being efficient, effective and equitable organization that makes best use of the resources entrusted to us to deliver on our impact. We assess the health of our network, our ways of working, the progress on our strategic directions and our future-oriented organizational development.

Collaboration: FOCARE believes in the power of collaboration for greater impact and is committed to strengthening how we partner and engage with others.We work, those who mobilize with us to influence change and the governments that host us – and how well we enable and unleash the potential of these capacity of evaluate the relevance and strength of our collaboration- the wide range of actors and communities with whom we partner, those who fund ourpeople living in or originating from Teso – Uganda in such a way that they are better able to identify and help meet their needs and to participate more fully in society to achieve sustainable development collaborations.
